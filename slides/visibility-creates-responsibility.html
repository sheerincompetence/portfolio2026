Then reality arrived

Users treated every item in the research feed as a task. A feed isn't neutral. It creates a psychological contract: "This is being shown to me because I should care."

  • Employee uses phone
  • Customer lingers near exit
  • Stock room door open

The insight

Visibility Creates Responsibility

The problem wasn't the quantity of information. It was how visibility was interpreted.

What we expected

Two modes for two different jobs

Research feed → discovery. Triage feed → action. Information could exist in a neutral state.

What happened

Every visible event became a request for attention

Real operational data turned informational events into cognitive debt. Every item demanded judgement.
