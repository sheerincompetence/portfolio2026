Directions

Room I

The feed worked perfectly.

Accurate summaries. Trusted AI. And yet — something was structurally wrong.

Artefact 01 · Scale Five clips per camera per day. Two hundred always-on devices. The pipeline from sensor to human decision-maker was never designed for this throughput.

Room II

The wrong genre.

Everyone builds a datagrid. We built the best one we could.

Artefact 02 · Industry default Rows, columns, filters — the spreadsheet metaphor applied to continuous video evidence. Competent. Wrong.
Artefact 03 · The pivot From datagrid to feed. A social rhythm for operational stakes — scroll, scan, triage.

Room III

Making AI legible.

Every field on a card is a contract between model and operator.

Artefact 04 · Contract Severity, category, confidence — each label implies an action. Trust infrastructure, not decoration.
Artefact 05 · Dual modes Same organisation. Same day. Chronological for audit; priority for triage. Different job to be done.

Interlude

Synthetic data lied.

Actors. Office CCTV. It looked great. Then we turned the cameras on.

Vitrine · Production data

"It's overwhelming."

Room IV

Three types of wrong.

Artefact 06 · Taxonomy False positives. True but trivial. True and important — lost in volume. Unpicking wrongness before fixing the feed.

Visibility creates obligation.

Room V

The feed shrinks.

The product expands elsewhere.

Artefact 07 · Resolution Dashboards for patterns. Summaries for the day. The feed becomes a pointer, not a warehouse.

I went in believing the challenge was trust. I left believing the harder problem is deciding what deserves a person's attention in the first place.
